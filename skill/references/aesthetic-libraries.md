# Aesthetic Libraries

Genre-by-genre starter blocks for the STYLE block in a chapter image
prompt. Copy + adapt — these are openers, not full prompts.

The model handles named films / DPs / painters / photographers
reliably. It does NOT handle "make it look cool" or "trendy" or
"like Instagram." Names are how you talk to it.

---

## Thriller / conspiracy / historical mystery

```
STYLE: Da Vinci Code / Angels & Demons book-illustration aesthetic.
Comp films: The Da Vinci Code (2006) artifact-discovery interiors,
Spotlight (2015) office lighting, Knives Out (2019) study-room
warmth. Lighting refs: Roger Deakins low-key interiors;
Caravaggio chiaroscuro; the Bourne Identity (2002) Paris night
sequences for cobblestone-and-streetlamp work. Subtle grain.
Photorealistic. No HDR.
```

Variants:
- For chase / action scenes: "Bourne Identity," "Spy Game (2001),"
  "Mission: Impossible — Fallout (2018) pursuit cinematography"
- For interior / library scenes: "The Da Vinci Code (2006)," "Goodnight
  and Good Luck (2005)," "Carol (2015) interior chiaroscuro"
- For cathedral / religious interiors: "Angels & Demons (2009),"
  "The Two Popes (2019)," Caravaggio paintings

## Memoir / literary nonfiction

```
STYLE: Annie Leibovitz portrait restraint. Walker Evans Depression-
era documentary authority. Joel Meyerowitz color and quietness.
Mood: hard-won truth — a person looking honestly at the thing they
spent years not looking at. Comp films: All the President's Men
(1976) office lighting, Manchester by the Sea (2016) interior
melancholy, Lady Bird (2017) suburban warmth. Photorealistic.
No filter. No HDR.
```

Variants:
- For city / workplace scenes: "Joel Meyerowitz," "William Eggleston,"
  "Saul Leiter color"
- For rural / Midwest scenes: "Walker Evans," "Robert Frank,"
  "Stephen Shore"
- For intimate / family scenes: "Sally Mann," "Tina Barney portrait
  photography"

## Romance / contemporary fiction

```
STYLE: Sally Mann intimacy. Petra Collins soft pastel.
Cinematography references: Call Me By Your Name (2017) Italian sun,
Lady Bird (2017) suburban warmth, La La Land (2016) magic-hour color.
Mood: longing, recognition, second chances. Photorealistic. Soft
grain. No HDR.
```

Variants:
- Beach / summer: "Call Me By Your Name (2017)," "The Talented Mr.
  Ripley (1999) Italian sun"
- Winter / interior: "Carol (2015)," "Past Lives (2023)"
- Urban / professional: "The Best of Everything (1959) Manhattan
  office portraiture"

## Fantasy / SFF (epic register)

```
STYLE: Lord of the Rings (2001) Rivendell interiors and Rohan
exteriors. Game of Thrones season 1 (2011) candlelit interiors.
Caravaggio chiaroscuro applied to medieval settings. Comp painters:
John William Waterhouse Pre-Raphaelite, Ivan Aivazovsky seascapes.
Mood: weighty, sword-and-stone realism, no Saturday-morning fantasy
saturation. Photorealistic. No HDR.
```

Variants:
- Court / political: "Game of Thrones," "Wolf Hall (2015)"
- Battlefield: "Lord of the Rings (2001-2003) Helm's Deep / Pelennor"
- Quiet / pastoral: "The Two Towers (2002) Rohan steppe, A Hidden
  Life (2019) alpine"

## SFF (modern / techno register)

```
STYLE: Blade Runner 2049 (2017) Roger Deakins cinematography. Arrival
(2016) Bradford Young palette. Dune (2021) and Dune Part Two (2024)
desert + interior. Mood: future-noir, sparse, weighty. Comp painters:
Edward Hopper for urban loneliness. Photorealistic. No HDR. No
chrome-and-neon clichés unless the SCENE block asks for them.
```

Variants:
- Space / cosmic: "Interstellar (2014)," "Ad Astra (2019)"
- Cyberpunk: "Blade Runner 2049 (2017)" + "Ghost in the Shell
  (1995) animated reference for color"
- Post-apocalyptic: "The Road (2009)," "Children of Men (2006)"

## Horror

```
STYLE: Jordan Peele Get Out (2017) and Nope (2022) for suburban
horror chiaroscuro. The Witch (2015) for natural-light period
horror. Hereditary (2018) for interior dread. Mood: wrongness in
ordinary places. Photorealistic. No HDR. No jump-scare composition
clichés.
```

Variants:
- Folk horror: "Midsommar (2019)," "The Wicker Man (1973)"
- Psychological: "The Shining (1980)," "Black Swan (2010)"
- Gothic: "Crimson Peak (2015)," "The Others (2001)"

## Children's / middle-grade illustration

```
STYLE: Quentin Blake watercolor sketch with thin black ink line.
Beatrix Potter for animal anatomy and softer palette. Where the
Wild Things Are (Maurice Sendak) for crosshatch shadow. Mood:
warm, playful, lightly anarchic. Hand-drawn — visible brushwork,
visible ink. NOT photorealistic. NO 3D rendering.
```

## Cookbook / lifestyle / nonfiction product

```
STYLE: Bon Appétit magazine food photography. Donna Hay clean
overhead-shot styling. Comp photographers: Mikkel Vang for
Scandinavian-modern interior, Romulo Yanes for studio food
photography. Mood: clean, aspirational, edible. Soft natural light.
Photorealistic. No HDR.
```

## Business / tech / self-help

```
STYLE: Inc. magazine cover portraiture. Annie Leibovitz CEO portrait
work. Mood: confident, present, no smarm. Hard light from a single
direction — corporate but not stock. Comp photographers: Platon for
power portraits, Mark Seliger for editorial portraits.
Photorealistic. No HDR. No stock-photo expressions.
```

---

## Crime / noir

```
STYLE: 1940s-50s film noir lighting — Double Indemnity (1944), The
Big Heat (1953), or modern revivals like Drive (2011) and Nightcrawler
(2014). Comp painters: Edward Hopper "Nighthawks" diner light;
Caravaggio chiaroscuro. Mood: streetlamp wet pavement, neon reflection,
the protagonist already in the corner the case is going to back them
into. Photorealistic. Subtle grain. No HDR.
```

## How to write a NEW aesthetic block

If your book is in a genre this doc doesn't list:

1. Find three to five films that share your mood. Not "movies you
   like" — movies that LOOK like what you want.
2. For each film, name its cinematographer if you know it. The model
   often gives a stronger response to "Roger Deakins on 1917" than
   to "1917."
3. Find one or two painters whose lighting matches. Caravaggio is the
   universal default for chiaroscuro; Vermeer for window light;
   Hopper for urban-American loneliness; Rembrandt for portrait depth.
4. State a mood in one sentence using emotional nouns ("grief,
   recognition, hard-won peace"), not adjectives ("dramatic, moody,
   epic").
5. End with the constraints: "Photorealistic. No HDR. Subtle grain."
   These three lines suppress the most common AI rendering tells.
